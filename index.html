My Professional Story

1. Curiosity Driven
Chapter One

Understanding Perspectives

From an early age, I have been fascinated by the idea that multiple perspectives exist and that these different viewpoints can coexist to make our world better. To understand all these perspectives, I have tried to remain as open-minded as possible and explore new experiences. I began by pursuing a deeper understanding of the technical side of things, so I started studying mechanical engineering. Although not my true passion, I learned how to engage with rigorous and analytical individuals and to work diligently on subjects that didn't fully captivate me.

2. Product Discovery
Chapter Two

SQUID Journey Begins

Unsatisfied with my studies and seeking a different environment, I discovered SQUID as an opportunity to learn from a new angle. At SQUID, I started supervising the SQUID app, a news aggregator active in more than 60 countries globally. My role focused on content supervision and managing partnerships with publishing partners. I gradually transitioned into product management, working closely with the development team to improve user experience and retention. After 1.3 years, I had developed clear ideas about the product's direction, and to fully commit to product development, I decided to relocate to the Stockholm headquarters.

3. Leading Growth
Chapter Three

Country Manager Era

After one year working part-time in Milan, I was promoted to full-time Italy Country Manager, with the mission to scale the Italian team from 2 to 7 people. I was also tasked with building the SQUID Business app from the ground up for the Italian market: establishing its identity, mission, go-to-market strategy, and growth plan. Over these 2.5 years, I realized I lacked the computer science skills necessary to follow development closely. This gap motivated me to pursue a master's degree in High Performance Computing Engineering, with a major in computer science and AI.

4. Technical Excellence
Chapter Four

Master's & AI Innovation

Starting with limited foundational knowledge, I committed two years to mastering the fundamentals of computer science, programming, data structures, algorithms, machine learning, and AI. Right before graduation, I tested my abilities at the Google AI Hackathon, where my team reached the final (top 4 out of 60), with Edvige—an AI-powered agent that can increase knowledge worker productivity by up to 8 hours per week. I believe what distinguished our team was the product's clear identity, thoughtful design, and compelling mission that resonated with the judges.
